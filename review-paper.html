CYBERSECURITY IoT & AI

Intrusion Detection Systems in the Era of IoT and AI: A Comprehensive Review of Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Blockchain and Hybrid Approaches

Survey paper examining how the proliferation of IoT devices has reshaped the threat landscape and why modern IDS must be intelligent, adaptive, and explainable—covering classical ML, deep learning, blockchain-enabled security, federated/edge computing, and benchmark datasets through 2025 literature.

> ABSTRACT (SUMMARY)

The growing number of IoT devices has significantly changed cybersecurity, making intrusion detection systems (IDS) essential for network security exploration. This paper provides a comprehensive review from traditional signature- and anomaly-based methods to modern ML/DL, blockchain, and hybrid IDS grounded on DL and blockchain architectures that address latency, scalability, and adversarial challenges in real-time IoT/IIoT settings.

> PAPER STRUCTURE

  • Section II — Background: IDS taxonomy (NIDS/HIDS), signature vs. anomaly detection, IoT/IIoT/MT/WSN/edge computing, major evaluation criteria and datasets
  • Section III–VI — ML-grounded, DL-grounded, blockchain-grounded, and allied models (independent & hybrid)
  • Section VII — Comparative analysis of methods, datasets, and performance metrics
  • Section VIII–IX — Challenges, future directions, conclusion

> DATASETS & METHODS HIGHLIGHTED

  • Benchmarks: NSL-KDD, UNSW-NB15, CIC-IDS2017, CICIoT2023, Edge-IIoTset, WUSTL-EHMS, ECU-IoHT
  • Classical ML: Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM, decision trees, ensemble and metaheuristic feature selection
  • Deep learning: CNN, BiLSTM, autoencoders, GAN-based augmentation, hybrid CNN–BiLSTM, Deep IoT IDS
  • Blockchain & federated themes: light-weight authentication, PBFT consensus, IPFS, FL-IDS, blockchain + XGBoost classifiers
  • Allied approaches: metaheuristic optimization, transfer learning, cold-start federated IDS, AI-blockchain-lightweight frameworks

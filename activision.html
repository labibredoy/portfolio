CAPSTONE — CSE 3200 GAN / 3D CNN

ActiVision: A Deep Neural Network and GAN-Based Framework for Real-Time Human Activity Recognition in Night Vision and Infrared Videos

Integrated Design Project / Capstone Phase I proposal (BAUET, Batch 16th). Converts daytime video datasets into synthetic night-vision, infrared, and thermal representations using CycleGANs, then trains deep models for accurate human activity recognition (HAR) in low-visibility environments.

CycleGAN Synthesizer Frame Preview Mode: INFRARED (INPUT)

> PROBLEM & OBJECTIVES

Accurate HAR in night vision and IR video is limited by scarce annotated low-light datasets and poor illumination quality. The project leverages abundant daytime footage (e.g. UCF101, HMDB) and GAN-based image-to-image translation (pix2pix / CycleGAN) to synthesize night, IR, and thermal views before 3D CNN temporal modeling.

  • Select and implement a suitable GAN architecture for day→night conversion
  • Build datasets and perform HAR on original vs. converted video
  • Evaluate and compare recognition performance across visibility conditions

> SCOPE & APPLICATIONS

  • Security surveillance and military operations in darkness
  • Healthcare patient monitoring in low visibility
  • Autonomous navigation; workplace and laboratory night monitoring
TensorFlow / PyTorch OpenCV 3D CNN (ICCV 2015) RTX-class GPU training

> PROJECT INFO

Course: CSE 3200 — Integrated Design Project (Capstone Phase I). Supervisor: Md. Momenul Haque. Team includes Afia Mubassira, Md. Abdulla-Al-Labib, Md. Tarakur Rahman. Expected outcomes: night/IR datasets and a real-time HAR system for low-light environments.

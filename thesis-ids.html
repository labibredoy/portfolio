Thesis: Intrusion Detection for IoT Networks Using Intelligent & Hybrid Approaches

Ongoing thesis extending the published IEEE-style review on Intrusion Detection Systems in the Era of IoT and AI—from literature synthesis toward original experiments on modern IoT intrusion benchmarks, deep learning detectors, and hybrid blockchain-aware designs for real-time, resource-constrained deployments.

> ALIGNMENT WITH REVIEW PAPER

The review maps the evolution from traditional NIDS/HIDS to ML-, DL-, and blockchain-grounded IDS, emphasizing datasets (NSL-KDD, UNSW-NB15, CIC-IDS, CICIoT2023, Edge-IIoTset), class imbalance, concept drift, adversarial evasion, and federated edge deployment. The thesis targets identified gaps: lightweight models for IIoT, explainability, and hybrid DL + blockchain architectures with practical latency targets.

> PLANNED RESEARCH PIPELINE

  • Problem formulation from taxonomy and comparative tables in the review (Sections II–VII)
  • Dataset preprocessing on CIC-IDS / CICIoT / Edge-IIoT style flows with reproducible splits
  • Baseline classifiers (RF, XGBoost) vs. CNN / BiLSTM / hybrid stacks cited in literature
  • Evaluation: accuracy, F1, detection rate, false alarm rate, inference time on edge profiles
  • Exploration of blockchain-inspired integrity or federated training where feasible

> CURRENT STATUS

Comprehensive literature review completed (IEEE-format manuscript). Experimental framework, feature engineering pipelines, and model training are in active development. Final thesis chapters will report methodology, results, limitations, and deployment considerations for IoT-scale networks.

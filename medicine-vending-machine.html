EMBEDDED SYSTEMS CSE 4102 — MICROPROCESSORS

Smart Medicine Vending Machine

Self-service automated prototype built on Arduino Uno with a servo-driven dispensing mechanism, push-button input, and portable power—designed to dispense essential medicines efficiently where pharmacy access is limited (Winter 2025 sessional project, BAUET).

> MOTIVATION

Traditional medicine distribution often depends on direct human interaction, increasing delay and cost. This prototype demonstrates low-cost embedded automation for emergency or after-hours medicine access using minimal hardware and reliable control logic.

> HARDWARE DESIGN

  • Arduino Uno — main controller (5V operation)
  • Servo motor — rotational dispensing (signal on digital pin 9)
  • Push button / switch — user trigger (digital pin 2)
  • Power bank — portable 5V supply
  • PVC board & transparent plastic — lightweight enclosure
Prototype cost ≈ 1,880 BDT Arduino IDE

> OPERATION

On boot, the servo initializes to a home position. The Arduino polls the button; when pressed, the servo rotates to release medicine, then returns to default—looping for the next cycle. Literature review covered smart vending, pill dispensers, and IoT healthcare devices; future work includes cloud monitoring, RFID auth, and multi-compartment dispensing.
